Section 04

Pain Points

Platform Ceiling

White Fuse is rigid, poorly supported for SEO, and offers limited design customisation. This is the root cause of "very basic."

Credibility Gap

They work with FCDO, World Bank, Gates Foundation, UNICEF. Their site looks like a small local charity, not a trusted research org in 30+ countries.

Case Studies Are Buried

Their strongest asset (60+ engagements, 30+ countries) sits behind a flat list with no filtering, tags, or visual hierarchy.

Team Bios Lack Impact

Individual pages exist but are text-heavy with no visual consistency. Showcasing credentials is critical to winning contracts.

No Content Engine

No blog, no news, no RSS. Publications page is a static list. No way to surface content or signal activity.

No Audience Capture

No newsletter signup, no mailing list, no lead magnet. Every visitor is a dead end — zero owned audience being built.

SEO is Nonexistent

White Fuse generates poor markup, no schema, no structured data. For an org producing public research, a massive missed opportunity.

Poor Mobile Experience

White Fuse templates aren't reliably responsive. Stakeholders in low-income countries predominantly access the web via mobile.

No Social Proof Beyond Logos

No testimonials, no impact numbers, no partner quotes. The site tells but doesn't show results.

No Analytics Sophistication

Likely basic GA at best. No event tracking, no conversion goals, no visitor behaviour insights.

Section 05

Recommended Additions

Blog / Insights Section
High

You already produce the content — Learning Briefs, journal publications, conference presentations, policy recommendations. The problem is none of it lives on your site...

You already produce the content — Learning Briefs, journal publications, conference presentations, policy recommendations. The problem? None of it lives on your site. It all goes to LinkedIn or third-party journals, and your website gets zero benefit.

Every blog post becomes a permanent SEO asset that brings organic traffic from people searching for exactly the topics you specialise in.

It gives your LinkedIn audience somewhere to click through to, gives donors and partners a reason to come back, and signals that Jigsaw is active, producing, and leading conversations — not a static organisation with a static site.

  • Categories matching your four technical focus areas
  • Content automatically organised and discoverable
  • Your team writes a post, picks a category — the site does the rest
Evidence Library
High

This is the centrepiece of the new site — and your most powerful differentiator. You have roughly 100 publications that prove Jigsaw's depth and rigour...

This is the centrepiece of the new site — and your most powerful differentiator. You have roughly 100 publications that prove Jigsaw’s depth and rigour, but right now they’re scattered across external journals, partner websites, and a flat list on your current Publications page.

The Evidence Library brings it all together into a single, searchable, filterable hub:

  • Title, authors, date, and a short summary sentence
  • Link to the external source or a hosted PDF
  • Filter by region, author, and technical focus area
For a donor evaluating whether to work with you, this is the page that closes the deal — it shows volume, breadth, and academic credibility at a glance.

Built as a Custom Post Type in WordPress, your team can add new publications without touching code, and the filters update automatically.

Visual Identity System (No-Photo Approach)
High

Your photo policy is clear and well-reasoned — no identifiable faces without consent, no stock photos, no AI-generated images of people...

Your photo policy is clear and well-reasoned — no identifiable faces without consent, no stock photos, no AI-generated images of people. This is the right decision for an organisation working with vulnerable populations, but it creates a real design challenge: most websites rely on photography to create emotional connection.

The solution is a purpose-built visual system:

  • Your brand’s geometric patterns (the J repeat from the guidelines)
  • Data visualisations that tell your impact story
  • Abstract situational photography (landscapes, classrooms, technology without faces)
  • Consistent icon language across the four focus areas
This isn’t a workaround — done well, it becomes your visual signature. It makes your site immediately recognisable and unlike anything else in the sector.

This is a design deliverable we develop alongside the site, not an afterthought.

Impact / Results Page
High

You've worked in 30+ countries, partnered with 60+ organisations, conducted research with thousands of participants over more than a decade...

You’ve worked in 30+ countries, partnered with 60+ organisations, conducted research with thousands of participants over more than a decade. Those numbers are powerful — but right now they’re buried in copy or mentioned in passing.

A dedicated Impact page puts them front and centre:

  • Visual counters with animated numbers
  • Interactive map of countries
  • Key milestones on a timeline
Donors and institutional partners think in metrics — this page speaks their language.

It’s a living page that grows with every new project. When you finish a new study or enter a new country, one number changes and the page tells a bigger story. Low-effort to maintain, high-impact for credibility.

Newsletter Signup (Site-wide)
High

Right now, every visitor to your site is a dead end. They read, they leave, and you have no way to reach them again...

Right now, every visitor to your site is a dead end. They read, they leave, and you have no way to reach them again.

A newsletter signup changes that — even a simple “Subscribe for updates on our latest research” in the footer starts building an owned audience. Connected to Mailchimp or a similar platform, it gives you a direct channel to:

  • Share new publications and announce projects
  • Stay top-of-mind with donors, partners, and the research community
  • Build a distribution channel you actually own
LinkedIn is great, but you don’t own that audience — the algorithm decides who sees your posts. An email list is yours.

For an organisation that only recently moved away from Twitter, this becomes your second reliable distribution channel alongside LinkedIn.

Technical Focus Landing Pages
Medium

You have four clear areas of expertise: EdTech, Education in Emergencies, Education for Girls, and Education, Climate & Environment...

You have four clear areas of expertise: EdTech, Education in Emergencies, Education for Girls, and Education, Climate & Environment. Right now these are subpages with minimal content.

Turning each into a proper landing page does two things:

  • Makes your expertise tangible for a potential client who cares about one specific area — with context on why it matters, your approach, relevant team members, and auto-linked case studies
  • Creates four strong SEO entry points — someone searching “education technology research low-income countries” should find Jigsaw
Case study links are automated through WordPress taxonomies — publish a new case study tagged “EdTech,” and it appears on the landing page without anyone touching it.
Careers / Work With Us
Medium

You're a team of 21 that just expanded into Zambia. That's growth — and growth attracts talent...

You’re a team of 21 that just expanded into Zambia. That’s growth — and growth attracts talent.

A Careers page, even a simple one, does more than you’d think:

  • Signals Jigsaw is a real, growing organisation — not a consultancy that might disappear
  • Gives prospective researchers a way to express interest
  • Helps with recruitment when you do have openings — the page already exists and is indexed by Google
The alternative — no page at all — says nothing. And silence is never a good recruiter.
FAQ / How We Work
Medium

Potential clients often want to know: what does working with Jigsaw actually look like? How long does a typical engagement take?...

Potential clients often want to know: what does working with Jigsaw actually look like? How long does a typical engagement take? Do you embed with our team or work independently?

Right now, that information doesn’t exist on the site — which means it only comes out in a call or email. An FAQ or “How We Work” page:

  • Answers questions upfront and reduces friction for inbound leads
  • Pre-qualifies enquiries so conversations are more productive
  • Positions Jigsaw as approachable and transparent
For an organisation that works with governments and large INGOs accustomed to formal procurement processes, showing your methodology upfront builds trust before the first meeting.
404 Page & Site Search
Low

Small detail, big signal. A custom 404 page that points visitors back to useful content shows attention to detail...

Small detail, big signal. A custom 404 page that points visitors back to useful content (instead of a generic error) shows attention to detail.

A functional site search is essential for a content-heavy site — especially once the Evidence Library is live with 100+ publications and dozens of case studies.

  • Visitors who know what they’re looking for need a fast path to it
  • Low-effort, low-cost additions that quietly elevate quality perception
For an audience of researchers and institutional stakeholders who notice these things, it matters.
Jigsaw Zambia Section

You’ve just opened Jigsaw Education Zambia Ltd. with two new team members — that’s a milestone worth making visible.

A dedicated section signals:

  • Growth and local presence — commitment to the regions you serve
  • A home on the site for your Zambia team from day one

The cost of adding it now is minimal. The cost of retrofitting later is not.
Partner & Client Showcase (Redesigned)
Medium

Your current homepage has a basic logo strip — but you work with FCDO, World Bank, Gates Foundation, UNICEF, Save the Children...

Your current homepage has a basic logo strip — but you work with FCDO, World Bank, Gates Foundation, UNICEF, Save the Children, and the European Commission. That’s not a logo strip — that’s your strongest credibility signal.

A redesigned showcase:

  • Groups partners by type (bilateral donors, INGOs, international organisations)
  • Adds context on hover
  • Links directly to relevant case studies
When a potential client lands on your site, this is what converts them from “interesting” to “let’s talk.” Right now it’s an afterthought — it should be a centrepiece.
Accessibility & Multilingual Readiness
Low

You work across 30+ countries, your brand guidelines already include Tajawal for Arabic, and your audience includes government officials...

You work across 30+ countries, your brand guidelines already include Tajawal for Arabic, and your audience includes government officials and education stakeholders in low-income contexts — many accessing the web on low-bandwidth mobile connections.

Building the site with the right foundation from day one:

  • WCAG AA compliance for accessibility
  • Translation-ready architecture (RTL support, multilingual plugin compatibility)
  • Costs almost nothing if done from the start
For an organisation advocating inclusive education, an accessible website isn’t just best practice — it’s a statement of values that your partners and donors will notice.
