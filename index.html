Section 04

Pain Points

Platform Ceiling

White Fuse is rigid, poorly supported for SEO, and offers limited design customisation. This is the root cause of "very basic."

Credibility Gap

They work with FCDO, World Bank, Gates Foundation, UNICEF. Their site looks like a small local charity, not a trusted research org in 30+ countries.

Case Studies Are Buried

Their strongest asset (60+ engagements, 30+ countries) sits behind a flat list with no filtering, tags, or visual hierarchy.

Team Bios Lack Impact

Individual pages exist but are text-heavy with no visual consistency. Showcasing credentials is critical to winning contracts.

No Content Engine

No blog, no news, no RSS. Publications page is a static list. Client confirmed they won’t create blog/news content. Solution: LinkedIn feed integration + automated content spotlight for new publications and case studies.

No Audience Capture

No newsletter signup, no mailing list, no lead magnet. Every visitor is a dead end — zero owned audience being built.

SEO is Nonexistent

White Fuse generates poor markup, no schema, no structured data. For an org producing public research, a massive missed opportunity.

Poor Mobile Experience

White Fuse templates aren't reliably responsive. Stakeholders in low-income countries predominantly access the web via mobile.

No Social Proof Beyond Logos

No testimonials, no impact numbers, no partner quotes. The site tells but doesn't show results.

Limited Analytics

Google Analytics / Google Search Console confirmed in place. Needs proper event tracking, conversion goals, and visitor behaviour insights for the new site.

Section 05

Recommended Additions

LinkedIn Feed & Content Spotlight
High

Client confirmed they won’t maintain a blog or news section. LinkedIn is their primary and most active content channel...

Client confirmed: no blog, no news updates. LinkedIn is their primary and most active content channel — all activity happens there. The challenge is making the website feel alive without a content engine the team won’t maintain.

Instead of a blog, integrate a LinkedIn feed directly into the site and add an automated content spotlight that surfaces new publications and case studies.

This gives the site a sense of activity and freshness without requiring the team to create any additional content beyond what they already post on LinkedIn.

  • Embedded LinkedIn feed on homepage or dedicated section
  • Automated spotlight when a new case study or publication is added
  • Zero additional content workload for the team
Evidence Library
High

This is the centrepiece of the new site — and your most powerful differentiator. You have roughly 100 publications that prove Jigsaw's depth and rigour...

This is the centrepiece of the new site — and your most powerful differentiator. You have roughly 100 publications that prove Jigsaw’s depth and rigour, but right now they’re scattered across external journals, partner websites, and a flat list on your current Publications page.

The Evidence Library brings it all together into a single, searchable, filterable hub:

  • Title, authors, date, and a short summary sentence
  • Link to the external source or a hosted PDF
  • Filter by region, author, and technical focus area
For a donor evaluating whether to work with you, this is the page that closes the deal — it shows volume, breadth, and academic credibility at a glance.

Built as a Custom Post Type in WordPress, your team can add new publications without touching code, and the filters update automatically.

Visual Identity System (Mixed Media)
High

Client confirmed: real photos from their own work are welcome — no stock photography. The visual system will blend authentic project imagery with abstract design...

Photos are in — but only from Jigsaw’s own work. No stock photography, no generic imagery. The client will provide a bank of project photos, especially around case studies. The Dignified Storytelling pledge still applies.

The visual system blends authentic project photography with abstract design elements:

  • Real photos from Jigsaw’s fieldwork and projects (client-provided)
  • Brand geometric patterns and the J repeat from the guidelines
  • Data visualisations and interactive maps
  • Consistent icon language across the four focus areas
This mix gives the site warmth and authenticity that pure illustration can’t achieve — while maintaining the organisation’s ethical standards on imagery.

Client will curate and share their image bank so we know what we have to work with. This is a collaborative design deliverable.

Impact / Results Page
High

You've worked in 30+ countries, partnered with 60+ organisations, conducted research with thousands of participants over more than a decade...

You’ve worked in 30+ countries, partnered with 60+ organisations, conducted research with thousands of participants over more than a decade. Those numbers are powerful — but right now they’re buried in copy or mentioned in passing.

A dedicated Impact page puts them front and centre:

  • Visual counters with animated numbers
  • Interactive map of countries
  • Key milestones on a timeline
Donors and institutional partners think in metrics — this page speaks their language.

It’s a living page that grows with every new project. When you finish a new study or enter a new country, one number changes and the page tells a bigger story. Low-effort to maintain, high-impact for credibility.

Mailing List Signup (Site-wide)
High

Client has Mailchimp set up but doesn’t actively use it. They don’t want to call it a “newsletter” — framing is “sign up to our mailing list”...

Mailchimp already exists but isn’t actively used. Client doesn’t produce regular newsletters and doesn’t want to promise content they won’t deliver. The framing should be “Sign up to our mailing list” — not “newsletter.”

The goal is to capture engaged visitors so that when Jigsaw does want to share something (a year in review, a major publication, or an announcement), the list is ready and up to date.

  • Persistent “Join our mailing list” CTA in footer + homepage
  • Connected to existing Mailchimp account
  • No commitment to regular sends — used when they have something to share
LinkedIn is great, but you don’t own that audience — the algorithm decides who sees your posts. An email list is yours, ready when you need it.
Technical Focus Landing Pages
Medium

You have four clear areas of expertise: EdTech, Education in Emergencies, Education for Girls, and Education, Climate & Environment...

You have four clear areas of expertise: EdTech, Education in Emergencies, Education for Girls, and Education, Climate & Environment. Right now these are subpages with minimal content.

Turning each into a proper landing page does two things:

  • Makes your expertise tangible for a potential client who cares about one specific area — with context on why it matters, your approach, relevant team members, and auto-linked case studies
  • Creates four strong SEO entry points — someone searching “education technology research low-income countries” should find Jigsaw
Case study links are automated through WordPress taxonomies — publish a new case study tagged “EdTech,” and it appears on the landing page without anyone touching it.
Careers / Work With Us
Low

Not a primary page — footer link or sub-page only. Used ad hoc during recruitment rounds with a backend application form...

Client confirmed this is not a priority — the website is for clients and partners, not recruitment. However, Kara has used a hidden backend page in the past to host recruitment tests and application forms during hiring rounds.

  • Footer link or sub-page — not in main navigation
  • Backend page with form for recruitment rounds (activated when needed)
  • Not publicly prominent unless there’s an active opening
Low effort to set up, useful to have ready when the next recruitment round comes. Kara can update it independently.
How We Work (Dedicated Page)
High

Client agreed this should be its own page — separate from Services. Explains Jigsaw’s methodology, engagement model, and approach...

Confirmed as a standalone page — separate from the Services page. Potential clients want to know: what does working with Jigsaw actually look like? How long does a typical engagement take?

  • Engagement model, phases, and timeline expectations
  • Reduces friction for inbound leads
  • Positions Jigsaw as approachable and transparent
For an organisation that works with governments and large INGOs accustomed to formal procurement processes, showing your methodology upfront builds trust before the first meeting.
FAQ Page (SEO-Focused)
High

A robust FAQ page is currently the first page Google indexes for Jigsaw. It should be comprehensive, SEO-optimised, and cover everything about the organisation...

The FAQ page is the #1 indexed page for Jigsaw on Google right now. This means it needs to be comprehensive and written for search engines, not just users.

Content should cover everything about Jigsaw — services, Evidence Library, current projects, team, methodology — in a question-and-answer format. Even if it duplicates content from other pages, that’s intentional for SEO.

  • 100+ questions covering all aspects of the organisation
  • Q&A format optimised for Google’s featured snippets
  • Footer link — not a primary nav item (it’s for crawlers, not visitors)
The more comprehensive the FAQ, the better the entire site’s indexation and search visibility. This is a low-effort, high-impact SEO asset.
404 Page & Site Search
Low

Small detail, big signal. A custom 404 page that points visitors back to useful content shows attention to detail...

Small detail, big signal. A custom 404 page that points visitors back to useful content (instead of a generic error) shows attention to detail.

A functional site search is essential for a content-heavy site — especially once the Evidence Library is live with 100+ publications and dozens of case studies.

  • Visitors who know what they’re looking for need a fast path to it
  • Low-effort, low-cost additions that quietly elevate quality perception
For an audience of researchers and institutional stakeholders who notice these things, it matters.
Partner & Client Showcase (Redesigned)
Medium

Your current homepage has a basic logo strip — but you work with FCDO, World Bank, Gates Foundation, UNICEF, Save the Children...

Your current homepage has a basic logo strip — but you work with FCDO, World Bank, Gates Foundation, UNICEF, Save the Children, and the European Commission. That’s not a logo strip — that’s your strongest credibility signal.

A redesigned showcase:

  • Groups partners by type (bilateral donors, INGOs, international organisations)
  • Adds context on hover
  • Links directly to relevant case studies
When a potential client lands on your site, this is what converts them from “interesting” to “let’s talk.” Right now it’s an afterthought — it should be a centrepiece.

Asset status: Kara confirmed she has a folder with all client logos ready to share.

Multilingual Translation & Accessibility
Confirmed

Client confirmed: automated translation to Arabic, French, and Spanish. Primary language is English. AI-powered, no manual translators needed...

Confirmed feature. Client wants the entire site translatable into Arabic, French, and Spanish in addition to English (primary language). Translation will be AI-automated — no manual translators needed.

The system will:

  • Automatically translate all content when language is selected
  • Update translations when content changes (new pages, edits)
  • Support RTL layout for Arabic
  • Be trained to produce natural, human-quality output
This dramatically expands Jigsaw’s reach to local communities in the regions where they work — without any translation workload or freelancer costs.

Accessibility: Client wants our guidance on WCAG compliance. Happy to implement — details to be discussed with Becky.
