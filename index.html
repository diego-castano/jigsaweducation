Section 04

Pain Points

Platform Ceiling

White Fuse is rigid, poorly supported for SEO, and offers limited design customisation. This is the root cause of "very basic."

Credibility Gap

They work with FCDO, World Bank, Gates Foundation, UNICEF. Their site looks like a small local charity, not a trusted research org in 30+ countries.

Case Studies Are Buried

Their strongest asset (60+ engagements, 30+ countries) sits behind a flat list with no filtering, tags, or visual hierarchy.

Team Bios Lack Impact

Individual pages exist but are text-heavy with no visual consistency. Showcasing credentials is critical to winning contracts.

No Content Engine

No blog, no news, no RSS. Publications page is a static list. No way to surface content or signal activity.

No Audience Capture

No newsletter signup, no mailing list, no lead magnet. Every visitor is a dead end — zero owned audience being built.

SEO is Nonexistent

White Fuse generates poor markup, no schema, no structured data. For an org producing public research, a massive missed opportunity.

Poor Mobile Experience

White Fuse templates aren't reliably responsive. Stakeholders in low-income countries predominantly access the web via mobile.

No Social Proof Beyond Logos

No testimonials, no impact numbers, no partner quotes. The site tells but doesn't show results.

No Analytics Sophistication

Likely basic GA at best. No event tracking, no conversion goals, no visitor behaviour insights.

Section 05

Recommended Additions

Blog / Insights Section
High

They produce Learning Briefs, publish in journals, have deep institutional knowledge. A blog gives SEO fuel, return visits, and shareable LinkedIn content. WordPress posts with categories matching technical focus areas.

Evidence Library
High

Central hub for all publications: Learning Briefs, Policy Briefs, Year in Review, Research Reports. Filterable by type, technical area, country, and year. Downloadable PDFs with metadata. This replaces the current static Publications page and becomes the primary content showcase.

Visual Identity System (No-Photo Approach)
High

Due to their Dignified Storytelling pledge, the site cannot rely on field photography. Design a visual system using brand colours, geometric patterns, abstract illustrations, data visualisations, and icons to create visual richness without photos. This is a core design constraint.

Impact / Results Page
High

Dedicated page with key numbers: countries, organisations, studies, years. Visual counters or infographic layout. Donors respond to quantified impact.

Newsletter Signup (Site-wide)
High

Persistent CTA in footer + homepage section. Connected to Mailchimp or similar. Captures the audience they currently lose entirely on every visit.

Technical Focus Landing Pages
Medium

Each area (EdTech, Emergencies, Girls, Climate) as a proper landing page with context, auto-linked case studies, and relevant specialist team members.

Careers / Work With Us
Medium

Signals organisational health, attracts research talent. Even a "no current openings" page with a general application email is better than silence.

FAQ / How We Work
Medium

For potential clients: typical engagement model, phases, timeline expectations. Reduces friction for inbound leads and positions them as approachable experts.

404 Page & Site Search
Low

Custom 404 and functional search improve UX for a content-heavy site. Small effort, meaningful quality signal for credibility-conscious visitors.

Jigsaw Zambia Section
Medium

You've just opened Jigsaw Education Zambia Ltd. with two new team members — that's a milestone worth making visible. A dedicated section (even a single page initially) signals growth, local presence, and commitment to the regions you serve. More importantly, it gives your Zambia team a home on the site from day one. We build it now as a lightweight page that can expand into a full microsite later without restructuring anything. The cost of adding it now is minimal; the cost of retrofitting later is not.

Partner & Client Showcase (Redesigned)
Medium

Your current homepage has a basic logo strip — but you work with FCDO, World Bank, Gates Foundation, UNICEF, Save the Children, and the European Commission. That's not a logo strip, that's your strongest credibility signal. A redesigned showcase groups partners by type (bilateral donors, INGOs, international organisations), adds context on hover, and links directly to relevant case studies. When a potential client lands on your site, this is what converts them from "interesting" to "let's talk." Right now it's an afterthought; it should be a centrepiece.

Accessibility & Multilingual Readiness
Low

You work across 30+ countries, your brand guidelines already include Tajawal for Arabic, and your audience includes government officials and education stakeholders in low-income contexts — many accessing the web on low-bandwidth mobile connections. Building the site with WCAG AA compliance and a translation-ready architecture (RTL support, multilingual plugin compatibility) costs almost nothing if done from the start, but becomes a significant rebuild if added later. For an organisation advocating inclusive education, an accessible website isn't just best practice — it's a statement of values that your partners and donors will notice.
